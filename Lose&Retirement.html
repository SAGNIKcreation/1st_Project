Lonel Messi, the legendary Argentine footballer, has left an indelible mark on the sport throughout his career. His exceptional talent, breathtaking skills, and numerous accolades have solidified his status as one of the greatest players of all time. However, one elusive trophy seemed to consistently evade his grasp – the Copa America. After experiencing consecutive losses in the tournament, Messi decided to retire from international duty, marking a significant chapter in both his personal journey and Argentine football history.

Copa America Heartbreaks

The Copa America, South America's premier international football tournament, had long been a source of disappointment for Messi. Despite his immense success at the club level with Barcelona, the national team's inability to secure the trophy haunted him. Argentina had not won the Copa America since 1993, and Messi's desire to break that drought grew stronger with each passing edition.
In 2015, Messi and Argentina reached the Copa America final, facing their arch-rivals, Chile. Hopes were high, but the match ended in a goalless draw, leading to a penalty shootout. Messi stepped up to take the first penalty kick for Argentina, but his shot sailed over the crossbar, setting the tone for a devastating defeat. Chile claimed victory, and the agony of losing another final intensified for Messi.
The story repeated itself in the following year's Copa America Centenario held in the United States. Argentina again advanced to the final, this time against Chile once more. The match ended in a 0-0 draw, taking the game into extra time. However, Argentina's heartbreak continued as they lost the penalty shootout 4-2. Messi, distraught and visibly crushed, announced his resignation from international duty, an announcement that sent shockwaves through the footballing world.

Retirement and Reconsideration

Messi's announcement sparked an outpouring of emotions from fans worldwide. They pleaded for his return, recognizing the immense impact he had on Argentine football. Messi himself later revealed that his decision was made in the heat of the moment, driven by the weight of the defeats and his deep frustration.
Fortunately for Argentina and football fans everywhere, Messi eventually reconsidered his retirement and made a triumphant comeback to the national team. In 2019, he played a crucial role in leading Argentina to the Copa America semi-finals but suffered yet another setback against eventual champions Brazil.

Redemption at Last

After experiencing multiple Copa America disappointments, Lionel Messi's determination to secure the elusive trophy never waned. In 2021, fate smiled upon him as Argentina hosted the Copa America. The team's journey to the final was nothing short of spectacular, with Messi exhibiting his unparalleled skill and leadership.
In the championship match against arch-rivals Brazil, Messi played a pivotal role, setting up Ángel Di María's match-winning goal. Argentina emerged victorious, ending a 28-year wait for a major international trophy. The victory was particularly meaningful for Messi, as he finally laid to rest the ghosts of Copa America failures past.

Lionel Messi's consecutive Copa America losses and subsequent retirement from international duty were significant moments in his illustrious career. The Argentine superstar's determination to win the prestigious tournament remained unyielding, despite the heartbreak he experienced. Ultimately, Messi's redemption came in 2021 when he led Argentina to victory in the Copa America, a triumph that solidified his place among the footballing greats and brought joy to millions of fans worldwide.
